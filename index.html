SCARA Robot Writing Clock

A servo-driven SCARA arm that wrote (and erased) the current time on a whiteboard once per minute — scrappy, but it worked.

2019
CU Boulder • Dorm Build
Freshman Class Project • Solo

Project Overview

Live demo: the arm erases the old time, writes the new time, and waits.

What I Built

A two-link SCARA-style robot made from whatever I could find: a scrap wood base, tape, and aluminum bar stock pulled from the trash. A dry-erase marker sat in a simple plunger that could pick up the eraser, wipe the board, then write the next minute’s time.

Why It’s Here

This isn’t a showcase of refined engineering — it’s proof that scrappy systems, a bit of math, and stubbornness can produce delightful results. I wrote my own inverse kinematics and a mini “G-code” to command XY coordinates, then streamed paths to draw digits reliably.

Built before I knew CAD or 3D printing. I’m not ashamed — it shipped (to my dorm wall).

Technologies & Tools

C++ (Arduino) Inverse Kinematics Custom “G-code” Servos Whiteboard Mechanics

Technical Notes

Control & Kinematics

I implemented planar 2-link inverse kinematics for a SCARA layout to convert XY targets into shoulder/elbow servo angles. A tiny parser accepted lines of my “G-code” (e.g., MOVE X,Y, PEN UP/DOWN, ERASE), interpolated segments, and scheduled servo motion.

Coordinate path & simple command stream used to write digits.

Electronics (Very Simple)

Standard hobby servos driven by an Arduino. A basic driver board powered the servos; the marker plunger shared the same control loop. Nothing fancy — just reliable timing and careful movement limits.

Minimal servo control schematic — keep timing clean and power rails happy.

Results & Lessons

What Worked

It reliably erased and rewrote the time every minute. The inverse kinematics were stable, and the ad-hoc “G-code” made it easy to author digit strokes and erasing paths.

What I’d Improve Now

Real linkages, a proper end-effector, closed-loop feedback, and a sane mechanical stackup. Also: parametric CAD, 3D-printed fixtures, and limit switches so it can home without drama.
